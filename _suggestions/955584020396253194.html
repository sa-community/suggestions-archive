Here's an idea to make a better way to allow analog joysticks to work for sa and non-sa users.
Instead of using variables, it should use an on-screen joystick. Here's how it would work.
You'd start by setting an area the joystick is on the stage, it can be an area, or on a sprite (the area will be the size of the sprite, and be positioned in the middle of it). With the sprite position, it would follow the sprite.
Then when using it, it'll work by getting the position of the stick, and position the mouse in according to that spot on the stage inside the area you chose. It'll have an option to automatically click the mouse, or not when the stick is in not the neutral position. This is because sometimes you don't want the mouse to be down.
This, of course, will also be able to be saved in a project to allow creators to choose the area the joystick is in. It'll also remember your last setup so when you accidentally reload, you won't have to map the area again.
If you do this, then griffpatch's joystick tutorial will help scratchers to add analog joysticks to their games, and allow sa-users to use real joysticks.

The reason I chose to be able to position it on a sprite is so you can just position the area on an on screen joystick without having to worry about getting the size wrong. Another reason is some games that have mobile support, have you move according to the players position. This will allow you to play more games without any issues.
